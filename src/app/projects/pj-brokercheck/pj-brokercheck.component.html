BrokerCheck

FINRA

BrokerCheck is a free tool from FINRA that can help you research the professional backgrounds of 629,847 brokers and 3,607 brokerage firms, as well as investment adviser firms and advisers.

Where BrokerCheck Information Comes From

The information about brokers and brokerage firms that you find in BrokerCheck comes from the Central Registration Depository (CRD®). All brokers must be licensed and registered by FINRA, and CRD is the securities industry online registration and licensing database, Information in CRD is obtained through forms that brokers, brokerage firms and regulators complete as part of the securities industry registration and licensing process. The information about investment adviser firms and representatives comes from the Securities and Exchange Commission's Investment Adviser Registration Depository (IARD) database.

What You’ll Find in a BrokerCheck Report

For Brokers currently registered with FINRA or a national securities exchange within the last 10 years, contains:

  • A report summary that provides a brief overview of the broker and his or her credentials. A broker qualifications section that includes a listing of the broker's current registrations or licenses, if any, and industry exams he or she has passed.
  • A registration and employment history section that consists of: A list of registered securities firms where the broker is currently and/or was previously registered.
  • The broker's employment history for the last 10 years—both in and outside the securities industry—as reported by the broker on his or her last Form U4 (which securities firms use to register brokers with self-regulatory organizations (SROs) and states).
  • Employment history includes full and part-time work, self-employment, military service, unemployment, and full-time education. A disclosure section that includes information about customer disputes, disciplinary events and financial matters on the broker's record. Some of these items may involve pending actions or allegations that have not been resolved or proven. The broker's most recently submitted comment(s), if any.

For Brokers whose registration terminated more than 10 years contains everything above, as well as:

  • The subject of a final regulatory action,
  • Convicted of or pled guilty or no contest to certain crimes,
  • Subject to a civil injunction involving investment-related activityD or found in a civil court to have been involved in a violation of investment-related statutes or regulations,
  • Named as a respondent or defendant in an arbitration or civil litigation in which the broker was alleged to have committed a sales practice violation, and which resulted in an award or civil judgment against the broker,
  • It is within the long term product pipeline to include the broker’s most recently submitted comment(s), if any. In BrokerCheck platform as well, so there are both sides of stories prerseented to investors.

Design Iterations


Usability Testing

After finishing design, we tested compliance professionals and individual investors with this new version of BrokerCheck tool, featuring updated versions of the search results page and the broker summary page.

The objectives of the user testing were to:

  • Gauge receptivity to the new design among representatives of member firms and identify any potential concerns about clarity and fairness.
  • Test the usability, clarity, relevance and appeal of the new design for individual investors.
  • Provide guidance to the design team for potential changes and refinements to the prototype.

Testing Method:

We conducted 6 remotely-moderated usability tests with selected FINRA registered individuals, primarily senior compliance executives at major firms. as well as a diverse sample of 10 individual investors in the United States who trade with registered brokers or advisers.

All interviews were conducted remotely using GoToMeeting conference technology.

Options for Primary Search:

Conducted A/B testing to observe if users tend to lookup primarily by

Testing Result

General Impression

All compliance officers were very familiar with the BrokerCheck homepage and praised its simplicity and ease-of-use. Investors on the other hand were generally unfamiliar with BrokerCheck, but appreciated the clean look of the home page.

Most investors found the search functionality intuitive. Investors also appreciated the description of FINRA in the lower left corner, along with the description of BrokerCheck in the lower half of the web page.

Test Findings:
  • More than 90% of user interviewed lookup individual record through BrokerCheck.
  • Most often, investor respondents stated that they would initially look up a broker by name and address.
  • Several compliance officers directly or implicitly expressed a desire to sort the search results by firm or location.
  • From data perspective, it is more straightforward to compare the same type of info together.
  • Several compliance officers stated that they liked that Version B of the search results page presented the type of disclosures a broker had. Others found this information unnecessary.
  • The appropriate term for “disclosure” raised a big discussion inside team. Used to be called “events to review”, but it was not clear for general public, even cause confusion even for professional users. In the end, we decided to go with the official term with a legend explaining it.

Desired Features:
  • Many industry professionals expressed a desire to be able to sort the results by firm or location.
  • While only the “tile” format was available for viewing in this prototype, some respondents stated that they would have like to view the results page in a list format. Some felt that a list view would allow more brokers to be visible on one page and therefore make it easier to find the broker they were looking for.
  • Investors assumed that they would be able to refine their search for a particular broker by firm. (future)

Crowdtesting

Through Crowdtesting, we identified several usability issues within the product in progress:

  • Took some time to figure out what to be filled in each field to run the search query (), as a solution, we put placeholder into each search field.
  • Investors in particular had a difficult time understanding the icons on Page C & B as many did not understand the technical meaning of the word “disclosures.”
  • Many of the investors interviewed were unsure of the difference between an investment advisor and a broker.
Design Decision
  • Adding Tooltip on cards & legend on footer to explain professional terms that investors are professionals are confused with, so we included a legend and tooltip in the website to explain "Disclosure", "BD", "IA", and "[Disclosure Types]".

 BrokerCheck Home Page
  • Ensure that all complaints, arbitration awards, and settlements are promptly and accurately recorded in a broker’s and/or firm’s CRD record(s);
  • Ensure that the data disclosed via BrokerCheck is, at a minimum, congruous with the most liberal state sunshine law;

  • Add statistical information on the BrokerCheck home page to allow an investor to put an individual BrokerCheck report into context (e.g., include statistics showing the total number of registered brokers in the industry and the total number in the industry with one, two, three, four, or more investor complaints on their record);
  • Open the entire BrokerCheck database to the public (e.g., academics and other third parties) to allow deep data analysis and development of quantitative and qualitative reports concerning brokers and brokers’ co-workers.
