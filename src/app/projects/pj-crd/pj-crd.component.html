Usability Testing on Saving Filters


The finalized filter design was well accepted by users (compliance managers) during our usability test, as the structure of our filter UI presentation is well fitting the userâ€™s mental model, they can easily associate their daily task with the filteration feature provided.

Wheras, the design process of saving filter and apply saved filters was not so smooth. We went back and forth several times to iterate on our final design deliverable.

1st Attempt
  •    Lack of Indicator of applied [Saved Filter]

  •    Extra click required to apply [Saved Filter]
2nd Attempt
  •    [Saved Filter] was not discoverable.

  •    [Update Filter] concept is hard to understand.

  •    Not scalable if many filters options are selected.

Finalized Filter Saving Solution

  • Put "Saved Filter" and "Filter" closer on the layout.
  • UI Tricks for saved filter option, so it is visible regardless the opening status of the filter panel.
  • Removed Update Saved Filter feature, to simplify the process by removing undemanded features away from the path.

Lessons Learned

  • For all design related problems, circle back to what users need and goals.
  • Understanding the data structure and information architecture is they key to the right solution.
  • All design decisions need to backed by evidence (Browsing traffic, Survey, Interview notes) justifying the design rationale.
  • Documenting the iteration process to keep track of all discussed solutions, their pros and cons, and especially why they didn't work out.
  • Loop users in all stages of the design procss, as they could provide insightful feedback with their in-depth engagement interacting with the system.
    • Personal Takeaway
  • Reconsider the importance between visual design and product design, Making things functional first, and then make things pretty.
