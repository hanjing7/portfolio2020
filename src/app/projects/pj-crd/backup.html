Background of Web CRD

The 1980's. it was very hard to register as a broker. Each exchange and state government had different rules and procedures for registration. A large firm would need to submit dozens of different registration forms to register a single employee.

The regulators got together and agreed on a uniform set of registration forms (Fun fact: the "U" in U4, U5, etc. stands for "uniform"). Better yet, in 1999, FINRA (then NASD) launched a website (WebCRD) which was a registration "one stop shop". This was a huge win for the industry. Since then the world kept changing and CRD kept busy adapting to the changes.

Nowadays, as users increasly expect enterprise/work applications to be intuitive and more like applications they use in their personal lives , users expect information to be readily available and easier to consume so they can perform their activities more efficiently. A redesign of the CRD is coming on to the roadmap.

Some feedback received regarding the existing system includes

  • Tired of maintaining paper copies of filings and getting ink signatures
  • CRD requires extensive training to use. Not intuitive
  • It’s not obvious which information in CRD should be of heightened concern
  • It’s difficult to manage all of the compliance tasks. Must check several areas of CRD

Goal of Redesign

Given the context above, the goal of our redesign is to:

  • Create a registration & licensing system that encourages information sharing and makes it easier for the industry to meet its registration & compliance obligations
  • Lay the foundation for an efficient, risk-based, and data-driven disclosure and regulatory review program for regulators.
  • Curate customized user experience to provide a more modern site experience to our users that makes it easy for them to do their work, allow users to customize their views e.g. An Examiner’s objective for using CRD varies from a RAD Disclosure Review staff’s use of CRD, pursue the notion of a portal experience – Firm access via Firm Gateway; FINRA access via Enterprise desktop etc.

Problems to be Solved

One of the most important tasks we try to resolve through new platform is to keep firm users updated with their activities requiring their attention. Highlight activities needing scrutiny. allow firms to filter/sort/export/manage tasks based on customized needs. Activity Types

Carrying knowledge from classic CRD platform, users typically open CRD to follow 6 types of activities:

  • Reminding firm users with upcoming, approaching and past due continuing education requirement
  • Managing disclosure events for Rep employees by providing draft, review and resolve
  • Notifying employee with their exam status
  • Onboard new Rep employee with registration, disclosure and fingerprint information
  • Update personal information, registration and termination record for Reps

By looking into historical analytics, we find the top activities by frequency of use are sorted as:

CE Requirement > Registration > Employment Info Update > disclosure event > Onboarding

Design Challenges

UI Challenge - Movable Piece

It has been discussed intensively regarding whether allowing users to drag and drop to customize their dashboard interface is necessary configuration of our platform. Feature of such has been applied to many FINRA systems and widely accepted by management as a common knowledge. However, through a couple of user interviews, we learned that most novice users doesn’t realize they can customize the UI due to lacking of visual indicator. From conversation with engineers we learned it is challenging to build such components that could scale easily. We start rethinking about the necessity of having this feature. After conducting some (comparative analysis)…. With similar enterprise level products, such as ... , we noticed less movable pieces being used in system design nowadays. Probably because the visual hint is so subtle to notice, and the strategy of allowing users to restore their previous setting would be a even bigger challenge. A good design usually doesn't require change of views very often, e,g. … I believe Movable pieces is great for expert user but not so friendly for beginners who are just started with the system. Once expert users get their customized view configured, they are less likely to use feature of such to modify the view either. Since the new web CRD is targeting at lower entrance barrier and flatten learning curve, building a generic usable dashboard rather than a configurable one should be our main focus.

Discussion around saved filters option

Numerous discussion has been undertaken regarding the appropriate set up of filters. At first it is straightforward that clicking on filter button would reveal the filter panel.

Hassles come in when “save filter” feature kicks in, as well as the option to apply an existing saved filter and update the current filter. This complicates the flow with far more requirements as when user trigger the filter, the UI need to clearly indicate if it is a saved one, if it has been modified, if the modified changes has been applied and how to rename this filter.

Option 1

The initial solution we had was to have the save filter feature inside the filters panel, as they are considered

Detail Improvements: Naming the new filter was a modal popup, that get changed into a field level update in later revisions to preserve the context action took place.

Option 2

We received some criticism during internal review for the access to saved filter is buried too deep, that it takes one extra click to open. Also the display of having dropdown control on top of panel yielded too many layers.

So we came up with another version with saved filters moved up on hierarchy. The logic behind such design choice being filters and filter options are under the same logic group, “saved filters” are the controls on top of filter settings.

Option 3

The design looks very good by mockup but was quickly falling down in later useability testing. As users were asked how would they save a filter or apply a filter that has been saved already, majority of them tend to look for options inside filter panel and ignore the “saved filter” text there right at their face. The reason for that was partly because two controls could be far away from each other on wide display that users can’t easily draw connections between the two.

Final Design Solution

At last, we came up with this composite design that balance the expectations from all parties. In this revision, not only we moved “save filter” and “filter” options close to each other, but also keep both of them visible when panel is revealed, not giving users a single chance to miss them.

Beyond that, this flow perfectly solved dilemma of multiple layering on UI as the overlay/confirmation modal from deletion is visually separated from the panel.

Impact of New CRD

This new and improved CRD will benefit everyone involved - The industry, FINRA and other regulators. Return on investment could be measured in terms of the significant time and cost savings for firms in the efficiencies gained with the improved channels of communications and ease of reporting with the new interfaces. Allowing firms to achieve greater levels of compliance at reduced costs, by lowering learning curves or reducing missed filings or deadlines that could otherwise result in costly penalties. Similarly, FINRA and other regulators benefit because there are efficiencies gained by not spending time and labour on manual processes but are freed to use them instead in more effective ways to monitor firms, providing savings that go beyond budget line items.
